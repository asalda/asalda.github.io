About Me

Hi, I am Alberto Saldana-Lopez, a Postdoctoral Fellow in Prof. Matthew Hayes' group at the Astronomy Department of Stockholm University (SU, Sweden). My research focuses on understanding the physical conditions that lead to the Reionization of the Universe a billion years after the Big Bang. In particular, I use space telescopes to study the interplay between the stars, gas and dust in nearby starburst galaxies, to decipher how the (elusive) ionizing radiation escaped from their distant counterparts during the Dawn of cosmic star formation.

Check out my research page if you want to know more about this topic!

Academic journey and CV

My career has been full of traveling! After completing my degree in Physics and my masters in Astrophysics (both in my natal Spain), I moved to Switzerland were I got my PhD in Astronomy at the University of Geneva (UniGE), under the supervision of Prof. Daniel Schaerer.

Here's a link to my PhD thesis, and a full version of my CV.

Teaching and outreach

Teaching is an essential parts of my academic endeavour. During my undergrad, I worked as a Physics, Chemistry and Math tutor. As part of my PhD, I carried out research projects with undergraduates. For my postdoc, I had the chance to supervise both undergraduate and PhD students, as well as to be part of graduate thesis committees. Recently, I have guest lectures for the Astrophysics Master's program at Stockholm University... I defend that Science must be accesible for the general public, for which outreach is a fundamental piece of my teaching philosohpy.

Find out more in the teaching section of this website!

Publications and data access

I have lead (5) first-author publications in world-class scientific journals, and participated in more than (50) peer-reviewed articles. I advocate for an ethical and transparent scientific methodology, where discoveries and applications must be visible and publicly available.

If you go to data access, you will find the some of the data products that came out of my research. A list of my recent publications can be found in the NASA/ADS database.

Coding contributions

I support open-source and collaborative programming projects. Among my software contributions, I have developed FiCUS, a python package for inferring the physical properties of galaxies with available ultraviolet spectroscopic data. The code has been well-received by the community, with more than 10 works making use of it to date.

Visit the coding section to learn more about FiCUS and other projects.

Free time and work-life balance

In my free time, I like to read (very much into Japanese mystery authors, lately), running, cooking (desserts, mostly...) and wood carving. If I happen to remember bringing the appropriate device, I document my travels through the lens of a camera!
