About Me

Hi, I am Alberto Saldana-Lopez, currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Astronomy Department at Stockholm University (Sweden), working in Prof. Matthew Hayes' group. My research focuses on understanding the physical conditions that lead to the Reionization of the Universe a billion years after the Big Bang. In particular, I use space-based observatories to study the interplay between the stars, gas and dust in starburst galaxies, to decipher how the (elusive) ionizing radiation escaped from their high-redshift counterparts during the Dawn of cosmic star formation. Check out my reseach page if you want to know more.

Academic journey

My career is full of traveling! After completing my degree in Physics and my masters in Astrophysics (both in my natal Spain), I move to Switzerland were I completed my PhD in Astronomy at the University of Geneva, under the supervision of Prof. Daniel Schaerer. Here's a link to my PhD thesis .

Publications, data access and coding

I strongly support open-source codes and collaborative programming projects. Among my software contributions, I have developed FiCUS , a python for inferring the stellar content and physical properties of galaxies with available UV spectroscopic data. The code has been well-received by the community, with more than 40 works making use of it so far.

Teaching and outreach

Teaching is a fundamental piece of my curriculum. During my undergrad, I work as a Physics, Chemistry and Math tutor. As part of my PhD, I had the opportunity to leading small research topics with undergraduate students.
