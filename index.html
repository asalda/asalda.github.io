About Me

Hi, I am Alberto Saldana-Lopez, a Postdoctoral Fellow in Prof. Matthew Hayes' group at the Astronomy Department of Stockholm University (SU, Sweden). My research focuses on understanding the physical conditions that lead to the Reionization of the Universe a billion years after the Big Bang. In particular, I use space telescopes to study the interplay between the stars, gas and dust in nearby starburst galaxies, to decipher how the (elusive) ionizing radiation escaped from their distant counterparts during the Dawn of cosmic star formation.

Research

I have lead (5) first-author publications in world-class scientific journals, and participated in more than (50) peer-reviewed articles, adding up to more than 250 citations. I advocate for an ethical and transparent scientific methodology, where discoveries and applications are visible and publicly available.

A list of my recent publications can be found in the NASA/ADS database.

The LaCOS survey: nearby Lyman-alpha halos with HST imaging

The circumgalactic medium (CGM) hosts a large gas reservoir around galaxies, whose kinematics and composition is linked to the physical properties of the galaxies themselves. In my most recent paper, we explored how the morphology and extension of the CGM, as traced by Lyman-alpha halos seen in emission, impacts the escape of ionizing radiation in star-formaing galaxies. You can learn more about the LaCOS survey by reading my paper!

A JWST view of feedback in high-redshift galaxies

Stellar winds and supernovae inject energy and momentum into the interstellar medium (ISM) of galaxies, triggering or preventing future star-formation events. This stellar feedback manifests in the form of galactic winds and has not been constrained yet in the more distant Universe. In this paper, we investigate the outflows and dynamical masses of high-z galaxies, in order to decipher whether or not the winds in these systems are strong enough to remain bounded to the hosts... Check out our manuscript if you are interested!

Absoprtion spectroscopy with the public ESO-VANDELS survey

VANDELS is one of the last ESO public spectrosopic surveys, targetting hundreds of star-forming galaxies at Cosmic Noon. In this work, we make use of this outstanding dataset to study the gas geometry and dust content of these sources, by looking at particular spectrosopic features seen in absorption in front of the star clusters (down-the-barrel). Our python code FiCUS was first presented in this paper too!

The HST Low-z Lyman Continuum Survey (LzLCS)

I am active member of the LzLCS, the most comprehensive survey studying the ionizing output of galaxies at low redshift. At the time of publication, the LzLCS tripled the number of Lyman Continuum (LyC) detections in the nearby Universe. In 2022, I developed the routines to measured the LyC escape fraction (fesc) in these galaxies, and established that neutral gas and dust contribute equally to the absorption of ionizing radiation in galaxies. The LzLCS has became a reference survey for the Reionization community, with many studies making use of our published high-level data products... Visit this link to learn more about the LzLCS!

Extragalactic Background Light

Processes inside galaxies emit radiation in a wide energy range, from ultraviolet (UV) photons released by the most massive stars, to infrarred (IR) radiation produed after the absoprtion of the same UV radiation by dust... In my first paper back in 2021, I constrained the spectral intensity of the so-called Extragalactic Background Light (EBL) and its evolution with cosmic time, by using the exquisite photometric ancillary data from the HST CANDELS fields. Take a look at our paper for more details!

Coding

I support open-source and collaborative programming projects. Among my software contributions, I have developed FiCUS, a python package for inferring the physical properties of galaxies with available UV spectroscopic data. The code has been well-received by the community, with more than 10 papers making use of it to date.

FiCUS

FiCUS is a customized Python script for FItting the stellar Continuum of extragalactic Ultraviolet (Uv) Spectra. The code was presented in Saldana-Lopez et al. (2023), but the methodology was first described and tested in Chisholm et al. (2019). A public, fully documented version of the FiCUS code can be found in my GitHub repository: https://github.com/asalda/FiCUS.
